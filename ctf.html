Caesar Cipher

A Caesar Cipher is a substition cipher where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a certain number of places down the alphabet.

Encryption

  1. Choose a shift value, often denoted as k.
  2. Replace each letter in the plaintext with the letter k positions down the alphabet.

Decryption

  1. Get the letter k
  2. Replace each letter with the letter up k times

Example

  • The plaintext word is "Cookies And Code".
  • We are going to encode it with a k of 10.
  • We then shift the alphabet down by 10 letters.
abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
klmnopqrstuvwxyzabcdefghij
  • Finally, match each letter with its shifted letter.
  • "Cookies And Code" becomes "Myyusoc Kxn Myno".
  • To decrypt this, we can either brute force different ks or have prior knowledge of the k's value.

Atbash Cipher

The Atbash cipher is a substitution cipher where each letter in the plaintext is replaced with its reverse in the alphabet.

Encryption

  1. Take the alphabet and reverse it
  2. Replace each plaintext letter with its letter in the reversed alphabet

Decryption

  1. Start with a reversed alphabet and match it with the normal one
  2. Replace each reversed letter with its letter in the normal alphabet
  • The plaintext code is "Hello World".
  • We are now going to reverse the alphabet.
abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz
zyxwvutsrqponmlkjihgfedcba
  • Match each letter with the reversed alphabet.
  • "Hello World" becomes "Svool Dliow".
  • To decrypt this, match the letters in "Svool Dliow" with its corresponding letters in the first row.
